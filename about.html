Our Story

From a community drop-in centre to a Sri Lanka-wide network

Community Strength Development Foundation (CSDF) is a non-profit, voluntary organization registered with the Department of Social Services. Inaugurated in 2002, it is registered in the National NGO Secretariat under the number L 67516 in 2003. CSDF is also registered as a company under Act No. 7 of 2007 as GA 2522. CSDF works independently, irrespective of race, religion, caste, and party politics, and focuses its services on marginalized women in Sri Lanka by providing the support services needed to uplift their lives.

In 2002, CSDF emerged as a Drop-in-Centre for sex workers and drug addicts in underprivileged communities in the Peliyagoda area, set up in response to a request from a team of marginalized women. In 2010, with the expansion of its activities, CSDF moved to Colombo 08, and its focus gradually shifted towards female sex workers and people living with HIV (PLHIV). Since 2002, CSDF has received assistance from several leading donors and has developed a network with more than 08 organizations across 10 districts.

Mission

To improve the health, safety, dignity, and rights of marginalized women in Sri Lanka through community-led services, advocacy, education, and sustainable support programs.

Vision

A society where every woman is respected, empowered, and has equal access to healthcare, justice, and opportunities.

Strategic Plan

A five-year approach for wider impact

With experience amassed over the years, in 2015 CSDF recognized its capacity to reach a wider range of marginalized women, beyond the smaller group of female sex workers it had been serving. Supported by an experienced and educated pool of resource persons, CSDF identified the need for strategic change. The objective of this strategic plan is to develop a five-year approach to widen activities for marginalized women in different social settings, drawing on the full experience of CSDF.
